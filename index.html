Biography

Background & Journey

I am an AI4X Principal Investigator / Postdoctoral Fellow at Nanyang Technological University (NTU) in Singapore, supported by both the NRF Postdoctoral Award and the NTU AI-for-X Postdoctoral Fellowship, a globally competitive top-tier Singapore government fellowship providing up to four years of support toward an Assistant Professor transition. I am fortunate to receive close mentorship and collaborate with both Prof. Weisi Lin (IEEE Fellow) and Prof. Dacheng Tao (IEEE Fellow) at NTU.

I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at Yale University working with Prof. Xiaofeng Liu and Prof. Georges El Fakhri (AIMBE and IEEE Fellow). Previously, I received my Ph.D. degree in Signal and Information Processing from the Dalian University of Technology (DUT, 'Project 985' in China) in 2024, where I conducted my research at the IIAU-LAB under the supervision of Prof. Lihe Zhang and Prof. Huchuan Lu (IEEE Fellow). I also obtained my B.E. degree in Electronic Information Engineering from DUT in 2019.

Beyond academic research, I am deeply passionate about AI + X entrepreneurship. I am a Founding Partner of X3000 INSPECTION (Startup, Series B Funding). From 2021 to 2024, I served as its Chief AI Scientist, leading the development of X3000Former, X3000NeXt and other foundational models for power battery generation and inspection, which have been deployed to serve top power battery companies.

We warmly welcome outstanding students to join NTU as my short-term or long-term RAs. Competitive and highly attractive compensation is offered.
X3000 INSPECTION is continuously recruiting top AI research interns. If you are interested, feel free to contact me.

Vision & Direction

Research Fields

My long-term research vision is to build a general-purpose visual intelligence engine under the AI-for-X paradigm. This vision is structured around three interconnected layers: Pattern, Unification, AI4X Applications. At the pattern level, I investigate self-driven learning mechanisms such as self-supervised, open-vocabulary, prompt-based, and in-context learning to establish scalable and transferable learning principles that reduce supervision dependency and enhance generalization. At the unification level, I study how diverse visual tasks, concepts, and modalities can be integrated within shared architectures and representations, enabling unified multi-task modeling, context-dependent concept understanding, and image-video multi-modal perception under a coherent semantic framework. Built upon these foundations, the AI4X application layer extends the engine into real-world domains such as healthcare and industry, validating its robustness in complex, domain-specific environments while continuously expanding the scope of X. Ultimately, progressing toward visual AGI requires iterative strengthening of learning patterns, deeper unification across tasks and modalities, and sustained expansion of AI4X domains to achieve multi-source input processing, multi-task learning, multi-modal fusion, cross-domain generalization, and a balance between expert-level performance and unified modeling. Recently, I have developed a strong interest in AI4ResearchTools, exploring how general visual intelligence systems can accelerate research workflows, and I warmly welcome collaboration and mentorship.
